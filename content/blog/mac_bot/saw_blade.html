What fits well in a mac mini? It turns the CD drive slot is the perfect size for a saw blade. Here are the results of the first brushless saw blade prototype. {%- endmark %}

Hobby brushless motors are a wonderous thing. This tiny 60 gram motor is rated for around 150 watts of power. What would happen if you hooked that up to a saw blade meant for cutting metal? It should make for a pretty mean combat robot weapon.

This first prototype is made from a 3d printed frame, a 5/16" bolt, and two abec 7 skateboard bearings. The two pulleys, linked together with a urethane belt, were made on a lathe using a button tool giving a gear ratio of ~2:1. Add to that the motor at 980 KV (rpm/volt) running off of a 11.1V lipo battery and the saw blade spins at ~5000 rpm. With full safety precautions in mind... we clamped the setup to a nearby metal railing and fired it up: frightening! The tiny brushless motor has no problem spinning up the blade to full speed - on the other hand the 3d printed frame and old railing were rather rattly; the blade and pulleys are likely unbalanced.

First up: piece of wood and some plastic - no problem. Aluminium - surprisingly easy... Steel plate - Wow! Not bad for a tiny little motor, 3d printed frame, and a harbour freight saw blade, and it sounds like a real saw blade.

Next up: get everything mounted inside the mac bot frame.
